About

Our social justice concern is how COVID-19 and the global pandemic impacted Americans' socioeconomic status. Because different people are affected by issues like the pandemic in different ways, some citizens suffer economic setbacks while others benefit due to their socioeconomic standing, our problem is significant. Understanding how those from disadvantaged backgrounds or lower income groups have endured worse economic outcomes than the upper class, who have prospered, is important. It is possible to address this problem by raising awareness of the difficulties that low-income people face economically, as well as by donating or decreasing costs in order to boost profitability.

We CARES

During Covid-19, an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, all citizens were advised to remain on lockdown unless there was an emergency. Due to these rules, the United States experienced a dramatic increase in unemployment. The government passed the CARES Act in an effort to help aid American workers, families, small businesses, and industries with fast and direct economic assistance. This Act was passed on March 25th, 2020, just about two weeks after the recommended lockdown. It has since helped give citizens on low and moderate incomes, and those who were unemployed the opportunity to obtain those goods and services needed for survival.
